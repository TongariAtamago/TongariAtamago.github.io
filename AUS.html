Australia
Australia is most economic country in ASEAN (the Association of Southeast Asian Nations).
In 2014, Australia Celebrated 40 years of partnership with ASEAN, and the country has a deep relationships
with ASEAN in a range of areas including security, culture, education, and development.
Australia has relationships without ASEAN; Japan, U.S., China, and South Korea.
Australia thinks best relationships with U.S., and the country thinks the area of Asian-Pacific is priority for diplomacy and trade.
Australian trade is most active with China. In exports and imports, China occupies top, about half exports and imports either.
Japan is important for Australia in machines. Australia has a biggest trade market with Japan, and both countries have been in welfare surplus.
In addition, 73% of Australia’s trade is with countries in the APEC (Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation) group.
